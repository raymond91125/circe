C. elegans Connectome · Community Detection

Four modules — one per functional domain.

Louvain modularity maximization on the weighted chemical + gap-junction network partitions the pharynx into modules that mirror its anatomy: a corpus pumping module, an isthmus peristalsis module, a terminal-bulb grinding module, and a systemic neuromodulatory group — reproducing Cook et al. 2020 (Q = 0.40 vs their 0.352).

SOURCE · cook_2020_pharynx (CIRCE KG) METHOD · Louvain (Blondel et al. 2008) GRAPH · weighted, chemical + gap, undirected

The module block structure

Cells ordered by module (then by degree); each square is the synaptic weight between two cells — darker is stronger. The dense blocks on the diagonal are the modules; the sparser off-diagonal shows they remain coupled, not isolated — the pharynx's shallow, interconnected architecture. (A force-directed layout can't cleanly separate a network this dense, so the matrix is the honest view.)

02

The four modules

Signature cells and function per Cook et al. 2020. Three modules coincide with the pharynx's three anatomical domains; the fourth is a systemic neuromodulatory group.

03

Is the partition real?

Louvain is stochastic and modularity has near-degenerate optima, so a single run proves nothing. Three checks: the partition is stable across seeds, its modularity beats a degree-preserving null, and the module count is well-behaved across resolutions.

Robustness

Resolution sweep (γ → modules, Q)

Reconciles with Cook 2020. At the coarse resolution (γ = 0.75) the network splits into exactly four modules at Q = 0.40, matching Cook's four functional modules (their Q = 0.352). At the default γ = 1 it resolves into six stable communities (Q = 0.32) — the extra splits subdivide the large corpus module; the grinding, peristalsis, and neuromodulation modules are unchanged.

Modularity Q = 0.32–0.40 is modest in absolute terms, consistent with Cook's own point that the pharynx is a shallow, highly interconnected network — the modules are functional domains, not electrically isolated circuits.

Methods & caveats

Data
Cook et al. 2020 pharyngeal connectome (cook_2020_pharynx), projected from the CIRCE knowledge graph: 55 cells. A single weighted undirected graph combining chemical synapses (both directions summed) and gap junctions; weights = EM serial sections.
Method
Louvain / fast-unfolding modularity maximization (Blondel, Guillaume, Lambiotte & Lefebvre 2008), as used by Cook et al. 2020.
Stability
200 random seeds; the community count and a pairwise co-assignment decisiveness score are reported; the maximum-modularity partition is shown. Deterministic (explicit seed lists).
Significance
Q compared to Louvain on 100 degree-preserving randomizations (double-edge-swap).
Caveats
55-node network; modularity has degenerate near-optima and depends on the resolution γ (reported as a sweep). Community labels ("pumping" etc.) follow Cook's functional interpretation.

C. elegans Connectome · Network Motifs

Reciprocal in pairs, feed-forward in triples.

The Cook 2020 pharyngeal connectome is significantly more reciprocal than chance — but that is a two-node property. Once reciprocity is held fixed, exactly two three-node motifs remain over-represented: the feed-forward loop and the reciprocal-pair-with-shared-target — reproducing Cook et al. 2020, Figure 6.

01

The finding, at two levels

Pairs — reciprocity is elevated. 40 of the network's dyads are reciprocal (chemical both ways), versus ~30 expected in degree-matched random networks (Z = +3.6). Reciprocity is 0.32 overall and 0.52 among neurons — several× typical somatic values, and genuine: 35 of 40 reciprocal pairs have asymmetric weights (e.g. I1L→I2L = 12 but I2L→I1L = 3), so it is not a symmetrization artifact.

Triples — the motifs are feed-forward. Testing 3-node structure against a null that also preserves the reciprocal-edge count leaves exactly two over-represented triplets: the feed-forward loop (030T, Z = +4.3) and the reciprocal pair → shared target (120U, Z = +2.2). Convergent, divergent, and chain fans are depleted.

The fully-mutual triangle is not a motif. There are 26 of them — but a reciprocity-matched null predicts ~24 (300, Z = +0.9). They are a shadow of the pairwise reciprocity, not an independent building block — which is why Cook et al. 2020 did not report them as enriched.

Why the null model decides this

A motif is structure beyond what lower-order structure already explains. Reciprocity is a dyadic (2-node) property; if the null doesn't hold it fixed, every mutual-containing triangle is trivially "enriched" as a mathematical consequence — not a triadic discovery. The standard for directed motifs (Milo et al. 2002; Cook 2020) therefore preserves the single- and mutual-edge counts, isolating true 3-node organization.

Under a weaker degree-only null (reciprocity free to vary), the fully-mutual triangle scores a spurious Z = +6.4 and the feed-forward loop looks absent — the reciprocity leaks upward and masks the real signal. The profile below uses the reciprocity-preserving null throughout.

Methods & caveats

Data
Cook et al. 2020 pharyngeal connectome (cook_2020_pharynx), projected from the CIRCE knowledge graph: 55 cells, 254 directed chemical edges + 32 gap junctions (25 chemical self-loops excluded).
Motifs
16-class Holland–Leinhardt directed triad census; chemical synapses as a binary directed graph.
Null — dyadic
1000 randomizations preserving in/out-degree only (reciprocity free); used to test whether reciprocity itself is elevated.
Null — triadic
1000 randomizations preserving in/out-degree and the single- and mutual-edge counts (Milo et al. 2002; the standard used by Cook 2020, Fig 6). Z = (observed − meannull) / SDnull; |Z| ≥ 2 flagged. Deterministic (fixed seed, sorted swap pools).
Reconciles
Two over-represented triplets, matching Cook 2020's Figure 6 count; the fully-mutual triangle is not enriched under the reciprocity-preserving null.
Caveats
A single 55-node reconstruction; Z-scores are indicative, not replicate-animal p-values. Edges are binarized.

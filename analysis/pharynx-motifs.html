C. elegans Connectome · Network Motifs

The pharynx wires for reciprocity, not feed-forward.

A triad-census analysis of the Cook 2020 pharyngeal connectome finds that bidirectional (mutual) connections and their clusters are strongly enriched, while directed chains, fan-outs, and even the classic feed-forward loop are not — a signature of a recurrent, rhythm-generating micro-circuit rather than a feed-forward information relay.

SOURCE · cook_2020_pharynx (CIRCE KG) MODEL · binary directed + undirected NULL · 1000 degree-preserving swaps
01

What the census says

Mutual structure dominates. The fully-mutual triangle (300, all three pairs reciprocal) is the single most over-represented motif — 26 observed vs ~7 expected, Z = +6.4 — followed by the reciprocal-pair- with-shared-target (120U, Z = +5.6) and the mutual chain (201, Z = +3.9). Reciprocity is 0.32 across the whole network and 0.52 among neurons — roughly 3–5× typical somatic-nervous-system values.

Feed-forward structure is depleted. Divergent fan-outs (021D, Z = −6.0) and directed chains (021C, Z = −4.3) are the most under-represented motifs, and directed 3-cycles (030C) are suppressed. Critically, the feed-forward loop (030T) — the defining motif of many neural and gene-regulatory networks — sits at Z = −1.5, i.e. no enrichment at all.

The pattern is identical when muscles and glands are removed and only the 26 pharyngeal neurons are analysed, so it reflects the neural wiring itself, not convergence onto effector muscle.

05

The gap-junction overlay

Electrical coupling forms a sparse, triangle-free mesh (32 edges, 0 closed triples, transitivity = 0) — an unclustered scaffold layered over the recurrent chemical core, not a second clustered network. I1R is its principal hub (5 partners), consistent with I1's role as the pharynx's main sensory-integrative interneuron.

Methods & caveats

Data
Cook et al. 2020 pharyngeal connectome (cook_2020_pharynx), projected from the CIRCE knowledge graph: 55 cells, 254 directed chemical edges + 32 gap junctions (25 chemical self-loops excluded).
Motifs
Dyad census and the 16-class Holland–Leinhardt directed triad census; chemical synapses as a binary directed graph, gap junctions as undirected.
Null model
1000 degree-preserving randomizations via directed edge swaps (Maslov–Sneppen), which hold every node's in- and out-degree fixed. Z = (observed − meannull) / SDnull; |Z| ≥ 2 flagged significant.
Caveats
A single 55-node reconstruction — Z-scores are indicative, not p-values from replicate animals. Edges are binarized (synapse weights ignored). Includes muscle/gland targets; the neuron-only panel isolates the neural circuit.
